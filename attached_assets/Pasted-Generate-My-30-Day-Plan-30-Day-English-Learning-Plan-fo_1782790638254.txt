# 30-Day English Learning Plan for Job Interviews & Professional Communication
## CEFR A1 Level | India Job Market Focus

---

## WEEK 1: Foundation Building – Daily Tasks (Days 1–7)

### Daily Time Commitment: 20–25 minutes

---

### **DAY 1: "About You" – Personal Introduction**

**Tasks (22 minutes):**
1. **Vocabulary Building (8 min)**
   - Learn 10 words with flashcards:
     - Name, Age, City, Job, Company, Experience, Qualification, Department, Role, Year
   - Write each word in a sentence (Indian context): *"My name is Priya. I am from Bangalore."*

2. **Pronunciation Practice (7 min)**
   - Record yourself saying: "Hello, my name is [your name]. I am a [job title]."
   - Listen to Google Translate's audio 3 times; copy the intonation
   - Save the audio file titled "Day1_Introduction"

3. **Writing Task (7 min)**
   - Write 3–4 sentences about yourself:
     - *"I am from Delhi. I am a software engineer. I have 2 years of experience."*
   - Check grammar using Grammarly (free version)

**Milestone:** Comfortable introducing yourself in 20–30 seconds

**Free Resource:** Google Translate (audio feature) + Grammarly.com

---

### **DAY 2: Job Titles & Roles**

**Tasks (23 minutes):**
1. **Vocabulary – Job Roles (10 min)**
   - Learn 12 job titles (India-relevant):
     - Software Engineer, Data Analyst, HR Executive, Sales Associate, Content Writer, Accountant, Nurse, Teacher, Manager, Intern, Support Executive, Trainer
   - Create a table: Job Title | Department | Key skill (e.g., Software Engineer | IT | Problem-solving)

2. **Listening & Repetition (8 min)**
   - Watch 2-minute YouTube video: "Common Job Titles in India" (search: "Jobs in India English")
   - Pause every 10 seconds and repeat the job title and one-line description

3. **Sentence Formation (5 min)**
   - Write 5 sentences using the structure: *"I work as a [job title] in the [department] department."*
   - Example: *"I work as a Data Analyst in the IT department."*

**Milestone:** Name 10+ jobs and describe your role in one sentence

**Free Resource:** YouTube – search "Common Job Titles in English"

---

### **DAY 3: Numbers, Dates & Experience**

**Tasks (24 minutes):**
1. **Numbers Practice (10 min)**
   - Learn counting: 1–100 (focus on pronunciation)
   - Learn ordinal numbers: 1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th, 5th… (for years)
   - Practice saying: "I have 3 years of experience," "I started in 2019," "I worked on 15 projects"
   - Use YouTube video: "English Numbers Pronunciation"

2. **Date & Timeline (8 min)**
   - Learn months: January, February, March… (focus on correct pronunciation)
   - Write your work timeline in English:
     - *"I joined Company X on March 15, 2021. I worked there for 2 years and 6 months."*

3. **Speaking Practice (6 min)**
   - Record yourself saying your complete timeline (joining date, duration, current status)
   - Listen and check clarity

**Milestone:** Confidently state dates, numbers, and years of experience

**Free Resource:** YouTube – "English Numbers & Months Pronunciation"

---

### **DAY 4: Common Interview Verbs & Action Words**

**Tasks (25 minutes):**
1. **Action Verb Vocabulary (10 min)**
   - Learn 15 work-related verbs:
     - Manage, Handle, Lead, Develop, Create, Analyze, Improve, Solve, Coordinate, Achieve, Complete, Design, Implement, Train, Support
   - Write each verb with a work example:
     - *"I manage a team of 5 people."*
     - *"I develop software applications."*

2. **Fill-in-the-Blank Exercise (8 min)**
   - Complete 5 sentences:
     1. *"I _____ the sales team in Q2."* (Managed)
     2. *"I _____ a new filing system."* (Created)
     3. *"I _____ customer complaints."* (Handled)
   - Use answer key on Day 4 completion

3. **Speaking – Sentence Building (7 min)**
   - Say aloud 5 work-achievement sentences using these verbs
   - Record and play back

**Milestone:** Use 10+ action verbs to describe work tasks

**Free Resource:** Linguee.com (Free – search for verb + "workplace")
